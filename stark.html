Open Organ
CreditHolt & Paulsen
This poster for the Stavanger Concert Hall organ performance (Orgelet i Stavanger konserthus) uses a dramatic contrast of light and shadow, framing the silhouette of an organist against massive, graphic vertical columns that echo organ pipes. The design blends photography, abstraction, and architectural rhythm, evoking the solemnity and grandeur of the organ as an instrument.

Line

Dominated by vertical lines, referencing organ pipes and suggesting monumentality.
Sharp edges and stepped pixel-like transitions add a digital feel to the physical space.
Horizontal line under the text balances the composition and grounds the typography.

Shape

Key shapes include elongated vertical rectangles and the organic silhouette of the organist.
Geometric shapes create a modern, minimal backdrop while the figure adds human scale.
The shapes act as architectural and symbolic structures.

Form

The combination of photographic shadow and vector-like shapes creates a layered sense of form.
The human figure is rendered in silhouette, framed by glowing light, giving a sense of depth and presence.
Abstract forms hint at the physicality of the concert hall environment.

Color

Extremely limited palette: deep navy/blacks, white, and warm light from the organ.
The use of darkness emphasizes quietude and focus, while white shapes command structure.
Color conveys atmosphere, reverence, and drama.

Value

Strong high contrast between white columns and dark background.
Mid-value glow from the organ illuminates the performer, providing visual hierarchy.
The contrast enhances the spatial and emotional gravity.

Texture

Mostly smooth, with subtle photographic grain in the background.
Contrast between soft light and hard edges adds tactile variation.
Texture contributes to the solemn and immersive tone.

Space

Powerful spatial illusion created by scale shifts between figure and columns.
Deep space implied by light recession and photographic backdrop.
Negative space on the left accommodates typography, balancing the layout.
