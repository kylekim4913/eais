The Sacred Dirt
CreditDentsu Inc.
This poster for the 105th National High School Baseball Championship (Koshien) presents a ghostly, emotional image of a baseball player captured mid-fall or in a moment of emotional release. The sepia-toned, textured visual and traditional Japanese typography evoke nostalgia, transience, and poetic reverence. It feels like a visual elegy—an homage to both the sport and the spirit of youth.

Line

Lines are mostly soft and blurred, due to the aged, photographic texture.
Implied diagonal lines through the figure’s limbs and uniform suggest motion and collapse.
Vertical calligraphic lines of text contrast the blurred imagery with precise strokes.

Shape

The main shape is the organic form of the athlete, isolated in a nearly abstract space.
Background texture forms circular and irregular patterns, resembling water, dust, or time-worn surfaces.
Shapes are undefined and dissolve into the background, reinforcing the fading memory aesthetic.

Form

The figure has a semi-modeled form due to lighting and grain.
Dimensionality is suggested by shadow and soft light, not sharp edges.
Form appears ephemeral, like it's disintegrating into the background.

Color

Monochrome sepia tones dominate—brown, cream, and soft black.
The color choice emphasizes nostalgia, dignity, and solemnity.
Absence of bright color reinforces the historic and emotional tone.

Value

Mid-to-low contrast image with a few deeper shadows (e.g., cap, shoes).
Value gradients add softness and a dreamlike atmosphere.
Typography, however, provides high-value contrast for readability.

Texture

Extremely grainy and mottled texture, resembling old paper or corroded film.
The visual surface feels tactile and timeworn, almost fossilized.
Texture plays a central emotional role, implying memory and erosion.

Space

Shallow spatial depth—figure floats without ground or horizon.
Space feels liminal and suspended, evoking a sense of timelessness.
Sparse composition and blurred background isolate the emotional core.
