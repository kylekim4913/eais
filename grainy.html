This poster for the 12th International Theatre Festival of the Blind and Visually Impaired utilizes a halftone portrait masked by a tapering geometric shape, framed within a vivid green field. The design merges experimental typographic layout, accessible contrast, and human emotion, conveying sensitivity and clarity while engaging with the theme of visual perception.
grainy
halftone

Line

Smooth curvilinear lines define the mask-shaped cutout revealing the face.
Fine dot-matrix halftone lines build the shaded face, mimicking vintage printing techniques.
Typography includes serif and italic styles, with a rhythmic vertical alignment.

Shape

The primary shape is the curved triangular cut that isolates the portrait.
Background shapes are minimalist rectangles, keeping focus on the central form.
The interplay of type and image shapes the page’s visual rhythm.

Form

Illusion of volume in the face created through halftone shading.
Background is completely flat, enhancing the dimensionality of the masked portrait.
Form is symbolic—hinting at identity, concealment, and emotional nuance.

Color

High-contrast neon green background against black and white halftone portrait.
Typography and logo elements are black for maximum legibility.
Color scheme reflects bold visibility and clarity, aligning with the event’s accessibility theme.

Value

Strong value contrast between green and grayscale elements.
Halftone texture creates tonal gradation within the face.
Value transition is used for emotional subtlety inside a bold overall palette.

Texture

Prominent halftone dot texture gives a tactile, print-like aesthetic.
The face texture contrasts sharply with the smooth digital green field.
Texture reinforces the theme of perception and visual decoding.

Space

Effective use of negative space around the image and text provides balance.
Mask shape creates visual depth, drawing focus inward.
Spatial layering is minimal but intentional, enhancing typographic clarity and impact.
